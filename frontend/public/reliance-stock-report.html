1. Overview & Key Stats

Reliance Industries (RIL) delivered a total return of +337.1% over this 5-year window (Nov 2015 – Nov 2020), growing from ₹456 to ₹1,993 — a ~4.4× appreciation. The period captured the Jio telecom revolution (2016–17), a major correction in 2018, a strong 2019 run, and a COVID-crash-to-recovery cycle that pushed RIL to then-all-time highs above ₹2,300.

3. 5-Year Price History

The chart below plots monthly closing prices from November 2015 to November 2020. Three distinct regimes are visible: a prolonged sideways/corrective phase (2015–2016), the Jio-driven re-rating rally (2017–mid 2018), a sharp correction (H2 2018), and a powerful recovery culminating in a COVID-crash-to-ATH cycle (2019–2020).

4. Yearly Performance

All six calendar years (or partial years) in the dataset were positive. The standout was 2017 (+69.8%) driven entirely by Jio's explosive subscriber growth rerating the entire RIL conglomerate. The weakest year on record was 2016 (+6.6%) as Jio's commercial launch was still pending and oil prices were depressed.

6. Volume Analysis

Daily traded volume averaged 99.8 lakh shares — more than 3.6× TCS's average over its 10-year period. Reliance is one of the most liquid large-cap stocks on NSE. Volume spiked dramatically in 2020 driven by rights issue excitement and Jio Platforms FDI announcements.

Key insight: Reliance's 5-year CAGR of ~34.4% is exceptional even by Indian large-cap standards. The Jio transformation (2017–2020) was the single biggest driver, turning a legacy oil-to-chemicals conglomerate into a diversified tech, telecom, and retail giant. The COVID period further accelerated this re-rating as global investors rushed to participate in India's digital economy story through RIL's unique portfolio of Jio Platforms, Reliance Retail, and the traditional O2C business.
The maximum drawdown of −45.1% (computed from the rolling peak over the dataset period, starting from Oct 2017 levels relative to the Nov 2015 low) reflects the significant volatility inherent in a commodity+telecom+retail conglomerate. Risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe ~0.92) are better than TCS's (~0.44) primarily because the high return more than compensates for the higher volatility in this window.
