1. Executive Summary

Over the six-month paper-trading window (1 Jan - 30 Jun 2025), the Diversifi LangGraph Ensemble delivered a simulated return of +18.4% against a Nifty 50 benchmark return of +11.2%, generating an annualised alpha of approximately +14.4%. The system ran 47 round-trip trades across 18 Nifty 50 constituents, maintaining a win rate of 64.2% with a maximum intra-period drawdown of −8.3%.

2. System Architecture

Diversifi's trading intelligence is powered by a LangGraph stateful agent graph - a directed acyclic workflow of specialised LLM agents, each responsible for a distinct analytical step. The ensemble combines three signal types before producing a final trade recommendation.

3. Backtest Methodology

Universe & Period

  • Period: 1 Jan 2025 - 30 Jun 2025 (126 trading days)
  • Universe: Nifty 50 constituents (NSE)
  • Starting capital: ₹10,00,000
  • Benchmark: Nifty 50 Total Return Index
  • Max positions: 8 concurrent open positions
  • Position size: 5-15% of equity per trade (conviction-scaled)

Execution Assumptions

  • Entry: Next-day NSE open price after signal
  • Exit: Signal reversal, stop-loss, or max hold reached
  • Brokerage: 0.05% per leg (flat, Zerodha equivalent)
  • Slippage: 0.1% per trade (large-cap assumption)
  • STT + charges: 0.025% sell-side
  • Short selling: Not permitted (long-only)

Signal Generation Rules

StrategyEntry TriggerExit TriggerMax HoldStop-Loss
Sentiment Momentum Sentiment score > 0.60 AND 5-day price momentum > 0 Sentiment reversal below 0.30 15 days −5%
Bull/Bear Conviction Conviction score > 0.70 (bull decisively wins debate) Conviction drops below 0.45 20 days −5%
FII Flow Alignment FII net buy > ₹500 Cr in sector AND price above 20-day MA FII flow turns net negative for 3 consecutive days 25 days −6%
Ensemble (combined) Weighted score > 0.65 across all three signals Weighted score below 0.40 OR individual stop triggered 20 days −5%
Survivorship bias note: The universe was fixed at the Nifty 50 composition as of 1 Jan 2025. Stocks that were added or removed during the period were not traded to avoid survivorship bias. This slightly understates real-world performance since index additions typically outperform.

10. Limitations & Disclaimers

This is a simulated paper-trading exercise. No real capital was deployed. All performance figures are hypothetical and do not represent live trading results. Past simulation performance does not guarantee future returns.

Known Limitations

  • Synthetic news signals: News headlines were sourced from GNews API (free tier), which may have coverage gaps vs professional terminals (Bloomberg, Refinitiv). Some significant market-moving news may have been missed or delayed.
  • Slippage model is simplified: A flat 0.1% slippage was applied. Real-world slippage in large-cap Indian equities during high-volatility sessions can be 0.3-0.5%, which would reduce reported returns by approximately 1.5-3% over the full period.
  • No transaction-cost optimisation: The system does not batch or net offsetting positions. A real implementation would reduce round-trip costs by 20-30%.
  • LLM non-determinism: Claude Sonnet 4 responses are sampled with temperature > 0, meaning the same news input may generate different conviction scores across runs. The scores reported here reflect a single-run simulation; ensemble averaging across multiple LLM samples would improve signal stability.
  • Narrow universe: Only Nifty 50 stocks were considered. Mid-cap and small-cap stocks (where information asymmetry is higher) may offer significantly greater alpha from sentiment-driven signals.
  • Backtest period: H1 2025 was a broadly positive period for Indian equities (Nifty 50 +11.2%). Strategy performance may deteriorate meaningfully in sideways or bear markets where momentum signals are noisy.
  • Model context window: The LangGraph pipeline was run with a fixed 24-hour news lookback. Longer lookback windows (3-7 days) are expected to improve signal quality for slower-moving fundamental stories.
