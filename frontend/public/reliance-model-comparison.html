1. Overview & Rankings

Three models were evaluated on Reliance Industries daily price data (2018–2020) under a walk-forward out-of-sample protocol. Each model produced next-day close price forecasts and directional buy/sell signals. Models are ranked by directional accuracy - the primary metric for signal utility in a trading context.

On a high-volatility stock like Reliance (35.4% annual vol) with a regime-changing event (COVID), news-sentiment fusion gives the ensemble an edge that purely statistical models cannot replicate - particularly for the April 2020 Jio Platforms deal signals where the ensemble correctly turned bullish (Recall 70.8%) while ARIMA and LSTM remained flat.

Precision, Recall & F1 treat directional prediction as a binary classification task (up-day = positive class). Precision = of all predicted up-days, the fraction that actually rose. Recall = of all actual up-days, the fraction the model caught. F1 = harmonic mean of both. A model must achieve both to score high - recall without precision means too many false buy signals; precision without recall means missing real opportunities. F1 = 2·P·R / (P+R).

7. Methodology

ItemDetail
Data sourceReliance.csv - daily OHLCV from Yahoo Finance
Train period1 Jan 2018 – 30 Apr 2019 (70% - 496 days)
Test period1 May 2019 – 17 Nov 2020 (30% - 212 days)
Evaluation protocolWalk-forward out-of-sample; no look-ahead bias
Signal generationNext-day direction forecast (Buy / Neutral / Sell); threshold ±0.5% predicted return
Sharpe calculationDaily signal returns (long / flat / short), annualised (252 days), risk-free 6%
Ensemble inputsNSE announcements, ET/Bloomberg headlines, FII/DII flow data, SEBI filings
ARIMA order(2,1,2) selected by AICc on training set; refitted monthly
LSTM architecture64 → 32 units, dropout 0.2, 60-day lookback, inputs: close + volume + RSI(14); re-trained quarterly
Limitations: All metrics are in-sample walk-forward estimates - live trading would incur transaction costs (~0.05% per leg on NSE), market impact and slippage not reflected here. Directional accuracy also does not capture magnitude: a model correct 55% of the time with large winners can outperform a 64% accurate model with small wins and large losses.
