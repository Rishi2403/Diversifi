The chart below plots monthly closing prices from April 2013 to April 2023, derived from the dataset. 4. Yearly Performance

TCS had 8 positive years and 3 negative/flat years over the decade. The strongest year was 2013 (+58.5%) driven by a weak rupee boosting IT export earnings. The worst year was 2022 (−14.7%) due to global rate hikes and tech sector de-rating.

Key insight: TCS has been one of India's most consistent wealth creators over the decade. Despite short-term corrections in 2015-16, 2022 and COVID, the stock recovered and made new highs each time except after the 2022 correction (which is still ongoing as of Apr 2023). The 10-year CAGR of ~16.7% substantially outpaces NSE Nifty 50's ~11-12% over the same period.
