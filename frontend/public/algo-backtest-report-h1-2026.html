1. Executive Summary

Over the seven-month paper-trading window (1 Jan – 14 Jul 2026), the Diversifi LangGraph Ensemble recorded a simulated return of +15.3% against a Nifty 50 benchmark return of +8.7%, implying an annualised alpha of approximately +11.4%. The simulation ran 52 round-trip trades across 19 Nifty 50 constituents, with a reported win rate of 61.5% and a maximum intra-period drawdown of −11.4%. The period was characterised by elevated volatility in Q1 (global trade uncertainty, INR pressure) followed by a partial recovery through May–July 2026.

2. System Architecture

The Jan–Jul 2026 simulation uses a modified version of the LangGraph pipeline from H1 2025, with three configuration changes: (1) multi-sample LLM ensemble (N=3 runs per signal, majority vote), (2) 48-hour rolling news lookback (up from 24 h), and (3) QuantConnect LEAN integration for event-driven execution simulation.

3. Backtest Methodology

Universe & Period

  • Period: 1 Jan 2026 – 14 Jul 2026 (147 trading days)
  • Universe: Nifty 50 constituents (NSE) - composition as of 1 Jan 2026
  • Starting capital: ₹10,00,000
  • Benchmark: Nifty 50 Total Return Index
  • Max positions: 8 concurrent open positions
  • Position size: 5–15% of equity per trade (conviction-scaled)

Execution Assumptions (LEAN)

  • Entry: Next-day NSE open price after signal
  • Exit: Signal reversal, ATR stop-loss, or max hold reached
  • Brokerage: 0.05% per leg (flat, Zerodha equivalent)
  • Slippage: 0.1% per trade (large-cap assumption)
  • STT + charges: 0.025% sell-side
  • Short selling: Not permitted (long-only)

Signal Generation Rules

Backtesting Infrastructure - QuantConnect LEAN

Trade execution simulation and portfolio accounting for this period were run through the QuantConnect LEAN open-source engine. LEAN replays historical NSE OHLCV data in strict chronological order - eliminating look-ahead bias and providing institutional-grade fill simulation including order queuing, partial fills, and realistic cost accounting.

In Jan–Jul 2026, FII Flow Alignment contributed more trades than in H1 2025, driven by higher institutional activity post-Budget announcements.

February's −6.5% drawdown was largely attributable to the Sentiment Momentum sub-strategy, which generated BUY signals in FMCG and IT stocks shortly before a sharp global risk-off event. The N=3 majority vote mechanism - new in this period - filtered out 3 additional borderline entries that the single-run H1 2025 configuration would have accepted, though it did not prevent the drawdown itself.

7. Risk Analytics

Drawdown Profile

The maximum drawdown of −11.4% occurred during February 2026, deeper than H1 2025's −8.3%. The primary driver was a global risk-off event (China macro concerns + US Federal Reserve hawkish commentary), which triggered FII selling across emerging markets. The ATR-based stop-loss - replacing the fixed −5% stop used in H1 2025 - likely activated earlier on some positions, though the final drawdown still exceeded the prior period, reflecting the severity of the February move.

11. Limitations & Disclaimers

  • Simulated data, not live trading: All results are from a paper-trading simulation using historical data. Live trading involves execution risk, market impact, and slippage that simulations cannot fully capture.
  • QuantConnect LEAN simulation assumptions: LEAN's fill model assumes trades execute at the next-day open price. In reality, especially during the February 2026 stress event, opening gaps could have led to significantly worse fill prices than modelled.
  • GNews API coverage gaps: News from tier-2 business sources, vernacular media, and analyst conference calls is not captured. Significant market-moving information from these sources may have been missed.
  • LLM non-determinism: Even with N=3 ensemble majority voting, Claude Sonnet responses retain some variance. A different seed run may produce materially different conviction scores for borderline signals.
  • Narrow universe: Only Nifty 50 stocks were included. The information asymmetry advantage that drives LLM-based alpha is likely higher in mid- and small-caps.
  • February 2026 event not forecastable: The global risk-off event in February was not predictable from the input signal set (domestic news + FII flows). A macro risk overlay (e.g., VIX threshold, global equity correlation alert) could have reduced exposure before the event.
  • Short selling not modelled: The long-only constraint prevents the strategy from monetising bear signals during drawdown periods.
